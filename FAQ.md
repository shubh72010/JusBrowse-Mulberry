# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Why not just use Firefox or Brave?
While Firefox and Brave are excellent, they are designed for the masses. Firefox (with RFP) is great, but its mobile version has limitations on how deep you can go into engine-level spoofing. Brave is Chromium-based, meaning it inherits the Chromium footprint. JusBrowse is built for those who need "best in class of customisation" levels of privacy on Android, with specialized features like **Airlock** and **Persona Isolation** that go beyond standard privacy modes.

### Can I sync my data?
Currently, **JusBrowse does not support cloud sync**. This is a deliberate security decision to ensure that no user data ever leaves the device. All your history, and settings are stored in an isolated, local Room database.

### Will you monetize the project?
JusBrowse is a non-profit, open-source project. There are no ads, no trackers, and no data selling. We believe privacy is a fundamental human right, not a commodity. But donation feature is planned.

### Is it stable enough for daily use?
JusBrowse is currently in **Alpha**. While the GeckoView engine is very stable, the JusBrowse UI and security layers are under active development. You may slightly encounter bugs or performance issues. But it's thouroughly stable enough for daily use.

### Can I use Chrome/Firefox extensions?
No.

### Why is the APK so large?
JusBrowse includes the full GeckoView engine (the same engine Firefox uses) to ensure we have total control over privacy. This adds about 100-150MB to the APK size. We use ABI splits to keep the download size for your specific device as small as possible. and we are working on reducing the size.
